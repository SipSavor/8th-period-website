The Official Constitution

The supreme law and guiding rules of our classroom simulation.

I. Chancellor and Law Rules

  1. If the Chancellor is not there one of the judges has to take over.
  2. Voting for another Chancellor must be 2 weeks, starting on the ending week on the last day (Friday) when school ends and ending on the Sunday of that week at 10:00pm so that you know who’s ruler on Monday morning.
  3. Have to have the popular vote to become Chancellor (Of everyone that voted).
  4. You can only be Chancellor for up to 3 terms consecutively.
  5. When the Chancellor proposes a law 51% of the people must say yes before it is passed.
  6. The ruler must follow all laws too.
  7. If 75% of the people vote yes to get rid of the Chancellor they have to leave office.

II. Judge System

  1. The Chancellor must appoint one person to become a judge; the people will appoint two more to ensure fairness. The people will appoint the new judges with a vote.
  2. The judges are there so if a new law is unconstitutional or violates the bill of rights it can be therefore thrown out.
  3. In order to get the judges to vote and throw out a law someone or people must tell them to.
  4. If over 75% of the people vote yes on getting rid of a person as a judge or getting rid of all judges that has to happen. It’s supposed to be hard to get rid of them.
  5. The defender has 2 minutes to convince the judge that he is not guilty. The accuser has 2 minutes to explain why he should be in prison or banished.

III. Bill of Rights

  1. Every citizen is allowed to complain about the chancellor or a new law without being sent to prison or being banished.
  2. If someone is voted “not guilty” they can’t be put back on trial for that same incident.
  3. The Chancellor has to publicly announce the votes of a law or judges to the class.
  4. You can’t be punished for something you did before that law was made.
  5. If you are “Guilty” your sentence for prison must be reasonable (not over 2 weeks).

IV. Laws & Sentencing

  1. Before becoming a prisoner or being banished the judges must vote and have 2 or more people say yes.
  2. When you are voted to be in prison you must be told how long by the judges.
  3. To get from being banished you must have over 66% of the kingdom's population vote yes to come back in.
  4. When voting you can't bribe people to make it turn your way otherwise you will be sentenced up to a week and a half of prison.
