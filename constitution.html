The Constitution

The supreme law and guiding rules of our classroom simulation.

I. Chancellor and Law Rules

  1. If the Chancellor is absent, one of the judges must take over their duties.
  2. Chancellor elections span two weeks, beginning on the final Friday of the term after school and concluding on Sunday at 10:00 PM, ensuring the new ruler is known by Monday morning.
  3. A candidate must secure the popular vote (a majority of all votes cast) to become Chancellor.
  4. A Chancellor may serve a maximum of three consecutive terms.
  5. A proposed law requires a 51% majority vote from the citizens to pass.
  6. The Chancellor is not above the law and must abide by all kingdom rules.
  7. The Chancellor can be removed from office if 75% of the citizens vote in favor of impeachment.

II. Judicial System

  1. The Chancellor appoints one judge, while the citizens elect two additional judges via popular vote to ensure fairness.
  2. Judges hold the power to strike down laws that are unconstitutional or violate the Bill of Rights.
  3. To initiate a judicial review of a law, a citizen must formally petition the judges.
  4. A judge (or the entire judicial panel) can be removed from power if 75% of the citizens vote for their dismissal. This high threshold ensures stability.
  5. During a trial, the defendant has two minutes to argue their innocence, and the accuser has two minutes to argue for imprisonment or banishment.

III. Bill of Rights

  1. Every citizen has the right to criticize the Chancellor or new laws without fear of imprisonment or banishment.
  2. A citizen found "not guilty" cannot be put back on trial for that exact same incident.
  3. The Chancellor must publicly announce the results of all law and judicial elections to the class.
  4. Citizens cannot be punished for actions committed before a law prohibiting them was enacted.
  5. If found guilty, a citizen's prison sentence must be reasonable and cannot exceed two weeks.

IV. Laws & Sentencing

  1. Imprisonment or banishment requires a majority vote from the judges (at least two out of three).
  2. The judges must clearly state the exact length of the sentence when condemning a citizen to prison.
  3. A banished citizen may only return if 66% of the kingdom's population votes to allow their reentry.
  4. Voter bribery is strictly prohibited. Violators face up to one and a half weeks in prison.

V. Amentments

  1. When another country starts a war they are instantly banished (To come back you have to go through normal laws).
